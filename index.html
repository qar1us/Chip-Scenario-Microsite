Chip Scenario

Compute as Strategic Cyber Resource

Compute becomes a decisive resource for cyber operations against highly strategic targets. As the capabilities floor rises and less sophisticated actors gain access to advanced cyber tools, vast compute reserves will allow nation-states to stay a tier above cybercriminals and less-resourced groups, and to outpace many AI-enabled defenders.

What compute enables

Specifically, compute enables a rival nation-state to:

  • Develop and train “cyber weapons-grade” models

    Purpose-built for offensive and defensive operations. Today’s frontier models with advanced cyber capabilities, such as Mythos or Opus 4.7, are trained as generalists. To gain an edge, nation-states will train or fine-tune specialized cyber models focused on vulnerability discovery, exploit generation, and attack automation.

  • Deploy AI systems against hardened targets

    Cyber task performance scales directly with inference compute. UKAISI evaluations found that increasing computational resources from 10 million to 100 million tokens improved autonomous attack performance by up to 59%. Other critical capabilities, such as discovering zero-day vulnerabilities, may be limited only by compute budgets.

A nation-state could use its compute advantage to attack highly strategic and well-defended targets such as military networks, critical infrastructure, and the energy and financial sectors. Compute could be used to train and run specialized AI systems that find undiscovered vulnerabilities in a target’s specific software stack and to run autonomous agents that continuously probe well-defended targets for weaknesses.

What this might look like

With additional compute, China builds AI systems trained for cyberattacks and deploys them at a scale the United States cannot defend against quickly enough. Thousands of AI agents work their way into the networks of major U.S. banks, clearinghouses, defense and aerospace firms, and other Fortune 500 companies.

In peacetime, Beijing uses that access to surreptitiously monitor American firms, stealing trade secrets. But in a crisis, China activates its access: a coordinated strike halts trading on U.S. exchanges, knocks out power across several states, and compromises additional critical infrastructure.
